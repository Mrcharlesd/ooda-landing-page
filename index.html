Four phases.
One lethal loop.

Each phase has a targeted set of questions designed to sharpen your thinking under pressure.

01 ——

O

Observe

Gather raw, unfiltered information from your environment without bias or assumption.

  • What is actually happening right now?
  • What data do I have — and what's missing?
  • What am I ignoring or filtering out?
  • What changed since last time?

02 ——

O

Orient

Analyze and synthesize what you observed through your mental models and experience.

  • What does this information mean?
  • What biases might be distorting my view?
  • What's the most likely scenario?
  • What are my options from here?

03 ——

D

Decide

Choose the best course of action based on your orientation — commit and communicate it.

  • Which option best fits the situation?
  • What's the cost of waiting?
  • Who needs to know my decision?
  • What does success look like?

04 ——

A

Act

Execute the decision with speed and precision — then immediately begin observing again.

  • Did I execute the plan as intended?
  • What new information is now available?
  • What's the effect of my action?
  • Do I need to loop again — faster?
